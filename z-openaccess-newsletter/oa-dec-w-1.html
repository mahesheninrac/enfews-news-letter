Dec 2024, Week-1

Open Access Newsletter

Growth & Capacity:

Installed Capacity:
As of November 2024, the installed capacity of Open Access projects in India has reached approximately 14.7 GW, showcasing sustained growth in renewable energy adoption

Leading States:
Karnataka remains the frontrunner, contributing about 33.5% of the national Open Access capacity. Gujarat, Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu, and Rajasthan follow closely, with these states continuing to drive substantial growth in Open Access installations, supported by favorable policies and increasing industrial participation.

Policy & Regulation: The updated Andhra Pradesh Integrated Clean Energy Policy (ICEP) 2024, effective October 2024, outlines strategies for boosting renewable energy adoption while ensuring grid stability. Key highlights include:

  • Renewable Energy Mix: Promotion of wind-solar hybrid projects, mini and small hydro, and innovative technologies like New and Innovative Renewable Energy (NIRE) technologies.
  • Energy Storage Systems: Accelerated deployment of Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) and Pumped Storage Power (PSP) projects to support grid reliability and ancillary services.
  • Green Hydrogen and Biofuels: Encouragement of green hydrogen production and advanced biofuel technologies with incentives for using agricultural residues and industrial waste.
  • Electric Mobility: Expansion of EV charging infrastructure with incentives for public charging stations and private operators.
  • Banking of Green Energy: Banking provisions for unutilized renewable energy, governed by the Andhra Pradesh Electricity Regulatory Commission’s updated regulations.
  • Renewable Energy Manufacturing Zones: Establishment of Renewable Energy Manufacturing Zones (REMZ) to support localized manufacturing of renewable technologies.
  • Project Timelines: Clear deadlines for project completion, reducing procedural bottlenecks under the "Speed of Doing Business" initiative.

Market Trends & Analysis:

  • Increased Renewable Energy Penetration by C&I Consumers: Installed renewable energy OA capacity reached approximately 14.2 GW as of October 2024, a growth of nearly 12% year-on-year (YoY) compared to October 2023. Solar dominated with 10.1 GW installed, followed by wind at 3.8 GW, and hybrid systems accounting for the remaining.
  • Shift Toward Group-Captive Models: Group-captive models contributed to 50% of new installations in 2024, up from 38% in 2023. This shift is driven by the waiver of CSS and AS under this arrangement. For instance, Maharashtra and Tamil Nadu reported a 15-18% increase in group-captive installations
  • Corporate Renewable Energy PPAs: The Corporate Renewable PPA market grew to approximately 7 GW cumulatively, with 1.3 GW added in 2024 alone. Large corporations such as Amazon and Infosys signed long-term PPAs for solar and hybrid energy systems, showcasing a demand increase of 20% YoY.
  • Round-the-Clock (RTC) Power Solutions: RTC solutions accounted for 30% of new installations in FY2024. Projects like Adani’s 700 MW hybrid setup cater to RTC demands, ensuring uninterrupted supply and enhancing the appeal of renewable OA.
  • Reduction in Cross-Subsidy Surcharge (CSS): States such as Gujarat and Chhattisgarh reduced CSS by 10-15%, making renewable OA more cost competitive. However, high CSS in Rajasthan and Uttar Pradesh remains a deterrent, with rates increasing by 8% YoY.
  • Digitalization and Smart Energy Management: Over 60% of C&I consumers in OA now use digital platforms for energy monitoring. Investments in such technologies grew by 25% YoY, improving efficiency and compliance tracking.
  • Expansion of Energy Storage:: Deployment of Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) grew by 40% in 2024, with 800 MW of storage capacity added under OA projects. States like Andhra Pradesh and Karnataka led these efforts to complement renewable sources
