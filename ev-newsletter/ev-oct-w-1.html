India Needs Improved EV Infrastructure for Public and Private Transport

India is promoting Electric Vehicle (EV) adoption but lacks adequate infrastructure. Experts at the Mobility Symposium highlighted frustrations with slow public charging, noting 180,000 EVs on the road versus 15 vehicles per charger. High costs and insufficient support hinder mass EV use. Transitioning to electric public transport is also challenged by high prices and cost-effectiveness concerns.

Analyst Insight
India is promoting electric vehicle (EV) adoption to combat pollution, reduce dependence on fossil fuels, and transition to a more sustainable transport system, despite the current lack of adequate infrastructure. This shift can lead to significant environmental benefits and align with global climate goals, ultimately fostering energy independence. To improve the situation, both the central and state governments need to enhance financing for EV infrastructure, focusing on expanding the network of fast chargers and incentivizing EV production and adoption. Additionally, addressing the pricing challenges will encourage wider consumer acceptance. JSW Pursues Strategic Alliances in Electric Vehicle Sector in India

JSW MG Motor Partners to Repurpose EV Batteries Efficiently in India

JSW is actively seeking alliances in the Electric Vehicle sector, reportedly in discussions with BYD's FinDreams for battery sourcing and Volkswagen India for technology & dealership collaborations. The project, starting with a 36kW UPS for an industrial site in Pune, showcases the potential of second-life batteries. This initiative, part of 'Project Revive', aims to provide sustainable energy options for SMEs & promote innovative battery management

India and U.S. Collaborate on battery mineral supply chain

India and the U.S. have signed a memorandum of understanding to strengthen supply chains for critical minerals vital for electric vehicle batteries, such as lithium and cobalt. Indian Trade Minister Piyush Goyal highlighted a partnership focused on open supply chains, technology development, and investment in green energy, involving mineral-rich nations in Africa and South America.

Analyst Insight
India and the U.S. signed a memorandum of understanding (MOU) to strengthen supply chains for critical minerals essential for electric vehicle (EV) batteries in a bid to enhance their partnership in green energy and reduce reliance on external sources. This agreement will foster collaboration in exploration, extraction, processing, and recycling of vital minerals like lithium and cobalt, promoting technological innovation and investment opportunities in both nations. The MOU not only bolsters domestic industries but also positions India and the U.S. as key players in the global green energy transition, potentially influencing global supply chains and market dynamics. Moreover, it opens avenues for partnerships with mineral-rich countries in Africa and South America, facilitating a more resilient and diversified sourcing strategy while contributing to sustainable development goals worldwide. → Read Full Insight

Hitachi Energy India Plans Rs 2,000 Crore Investment to Enhance Energy

BYD India Not Applying for EV Policy Benefits under New Policy

Hitachi Energy plans to invest Rs 2,000 crore over the next four to five years to enhance energy transmission and transition products. Focus areas include HVDC Technology, electric mobility charging solutions, and RE grid solutions. The investment aligns with Hitachi's 2030 growth strategy, emphasizing digitalization, local manufacturing, and collaboration with MSMEs to meet future energy needs.

BYD India has opted not to pursue benefits under India's new EV policy in the short term, focusing instead on homologation to meet demand for its eMAX 7 model, priced between Rs 26.9 lakh and Rs 29.9 lakh. While acknowledging the advantages of a manufacturing plant, the company will explore market opportunities without immediate investment, awaiting further developments.

Toyota Kirloskar acquires 827 acres for an Electric car plant in Maharashtra

BYD Pursues Manufacturing Partnership with Reliance and Adani in India

Toyota Kirloskar Motor has received 827 acers in Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar, Maharashtra, for a new electric and hybrid car manufacturing facility. With a planned investment of Rs 21,000 C, construction will start soon, aiming for production to begin in January 2026. The facility will produce 400,000 vehicles annually, creating 8,000 direct and 18,000 indirect jobs.

BYD is negotiating with Reliance Group and Adani to establish a manufacturing facility in India, aiming for regulatory support through their government connections. Reliance is exploring an EV factory with an initial capacity of 250,000 units, expandable to 750,000. BYD plans to launch a facelifted e6 and a sub-compact SUV by 2025, targeting the growing Indian electric vehicle market.
