Adani Total Gas Launches Hydrogen Blending System in Ahmedabad

Adani Total Gas has launched a Hydrogen blending system at its Shantigram township in Ahmedabad, blending 2.2-2.3% Green Hydrogen into piped natural gas for 4,000 consumers. The hydrogen is produced via electrolysis using Renewable Energy. The company aims to increase the blend to over 8% with regulatory approval, contributing to lower carbon emissions.

Analyst Insight
Adani Total Gas has launched a hydrogen blending system in Ahmedabad to enhance its piped natural gas (PNG) supply by incorporating green hydrogen, blending 2.2-2.3% of it into the natural gas for 4,000 domestic and commercial consumers in the Adani Shantigram township. This initiative supports green hydrogen production through electrolysis powered by renewable energy, promoting cleaner energy use while maintaining gas heating capabilities. As the company aims to increase the hydrogen blend to 8% or more, it encourages advancements in hydrogen production technologies, contributing to India's transition to low-emission energy sources. This launch not only aligns with India’s renewable energy goals but also opens avenues for increased demand for green hydrogen, stimulates investments in renewable technologies, creates jobs in various sectors, and fosters collaboration between public and private entities. H2e Power Systems Wins Tender for Green Hydrogen Project in Himachal Pradesh

Greenzo & RBM Infracon invest Rs. 200 cr Green Hydrogen in Gujarat

H2e Power Systems has won a tender from Oil India to develop a 1 MW green hydrogen project in Himachal Pradesh, expected to start in 18 months. Utilizing SOFC technology, the project will produce hydrogen at reduced costs. Additionally, the Indian government has launched the SIGHT program to support green hydrogen production, with significant budget allocations.

Greenzo Energy has signed an MoU with RBM Infracon to develop Rs. 200 crore green hydrogen facilities in Gujarat, with a total capacity of 15 MW across Jamnagar, Kutch, and Bhuj. The project will utilize Greenzo's indigenously developed electrolyzers and aims to promote sustainable practices while creating jobs and advancing India's clean energy transition by FY 2024-25.

Hardeep Puri Highlights Future of Green Hydrogen Energy

Ashok Leyland Unveils Plans for Hydrogen Truck Development

Union Minister Hardeep Singh Puri emphasized India’s commitment to Green Energy at the FT Live Energy Transition Summit, highlighting initiatives in Green Hydrogen and Electric Vehicles. He noted the goal to increase the natural gas mix to 15% and achieve 20% ethanol blending by 2025. Puri expressed confidence in India's potential as a global refining hub while reducing crude oil dependence.

Ashok Leyland, part of the Hinduja Group, plans to launch its first hydrogen truck within 18-24 months, as revealed by CTO Dr. N Saravanan. This follows the delivery of electric trucks to Billion Electric Mobility, part of a contract for 180 trucks worth Rs 150 crore. The company aims to produce 5,000 units annually at its Hosur facility.

India Invests $25 Million in Green Hydrogen Hubs

India is investing $25 million to develop hydrogen hubs at key ports to support green shipping, according to Union Minister Pralhad Joshi. This initiative includes bunkering facilities and $14 million for pilot projects under the National Green Hydrogen Mission, which aims for 5 million metric tonnes of annual production by 2030. India also seeks to become a top shipbuilding nation by 2047.

Analyst Insight
India's investment of USD 25 million in developing hydrogen hubs at key ports is aimed at advancing its green shipping initiative, part of a broader strategy to promote sustainable maritime transport. This initiative focuses on establishing bunkering and refueling facilities for green hydrogen-powered ships at ports like Deendayal, Paradip, and V.O. Chidambaranar, while also facilitating the transition of existing vessels to operate on green hydrogen or its derivatives. By modernizing shipyards and enhancing shipbuilding capabilities, the investment is expected to create over 600,000 jobs, stimulate economic growth, and significantly reduce greenhouse gas emissions in the shipping sector. Furthermore, this effort aligns with India's renewable energy goals, contributing to its ambition of producing 5 million metric tonnes of green hydrogen annually by 2030. Hyundai and NTU Collaborate on Hydrogen Energy Research

Oman’s Emerging Role in the Green Hydrogen Hub Market

Hyundai Motor Group has partnered with Nanyang Technological University (NTU) for a 3-year research initiative on Hydrogen Energy & advanced energy systems, announced at the Singapore-Korea Business Forum. The collaboration aims to develop sustainable energy solutions for carbon neutrality, focusing on innovative Hydrogen Production Technologies & establishing a tripartite research center.

The 'H2-diplo Decarbonisation Dialogue' in Muscat on October 16 highlights Oman's potential as a Green Hydrogen Hub, crucial for global decarbonisation. Key discussions will focus on policy implementation and Oman’s role in energy export to Europe and Asia. The event features expert panels and presentations, emphasizing the importance of Green Hydrogen for economic growth and climate goals.

Indian Railways Launches First Hydrogen Train in Haryana

Cabinet Approves Membership to Enhance Energy Collaboration

Indian Railways aims for net zero carbon emissions by 2030, introducing hydrogen trains as part of this effort. The first prototype will run on the Jind-Sonipat section by December 2024, with plans for 35 trains on heritage routes. Significant funding supports this project, promoting eco-friendly transportation and advancing India's green initiatives.

The Union Cabinet, led by Prime Minister Modi, approved India’s signing of a 'Letter of Intent' to join the International Energy Efficiency Hub. This move enhances global collaboration on energy efficiency and supports India's goals to reduce greenhouse gas emissions. The Bureau of Energy Efficiency will manage India’s participation, fostering advancements in sustainable energy practices.

Adani Merges Subsidiaries to Enhance Green Hydrogen Initiatives

Adani Group has merged two subsidiaries, Adani Infrastructure Private Limited and Mundra Solar Technology Limited, into Adani New Industries Ltd (ANIL), focused on Green Hydrogen and Wind Turbine manufacturing. ANIL, a wholly-owned subsidiary of Adani Enterprises, specializes in low-carbon projects and serves various sectors globally. TotalEnergies holds a 25% stake in ANIL.

Angola Launches First 600 MW Green Hydrogen Project in Africa

Accelera Opens 500MW Electrolyser Factory in Spain

CWP Global has partnered with Angola’s Sonangol and German firms Conjuncta and Gauff to develop a pioneering 600 MW Green Hydrogen project in Angola. Expected to produce 400,000 tonnes of Green Ammonia annually, the project aims to enhance Angola's energy security and export capacity. It positions Angola as a leader in Africa's green hydrogen industry.

Accelera by Cummins has launched a 500MW PEM electrolyser factory in Guadalajara, Spain, enhancing its European manufacturing capacity. The 260,000ft² facility will produce 5MW HyLYZER®-1000 systems and has the potential to scale to 1GW. It aims to create 150 jobs, increasing to 200 with growth. The plant also meets 70% of its energy needs through solar and geothermal sources.

Yara Opens New Ammonia Import Terminal in Germany

Plug Power Supplies 3GW Electrolysers for Green Hydrogen Project

Yara International has opened a New Ammonia Import terminal in Brunsbüttel, Germany, with the capacity to import up to three million tonnes of low-emission ammonia annually. This facility will support Germany's hydrogen economy and energy transition, providing around 5% of Europe’s hydrogen target for 2030, and enhancing Yara's role in decarbonization efforts.

Plug Power will supply 3GW of electrolysers to Allied Green Ammonia for a Green Hydrogen project in Australia, aiming for nearly one million tonnes of ammonia production annually. Set to be one of the largest green ammonia facilities globally, it will replace SMR technology with green hydrogen. Final investment decision is anticipated in 2025, with operations starting in 2027.
