MoP Revises TBCB Guidelines for Solar PV Projects

The Ministry of Power (MoP) has amended Tariff-Based Competitive Bidding (TBCB) guidelines for Solar PV Projects to improve compliance, grid integration, and efficiency. Key changes include specifying sub-station connections, stricter penalties for failing Capacity Utilisation Factor (CUF), expedited Power Purchase Agreements, revised Change in Law provisions, and financial flexibility through Insurance Surety Bonds.

Analyst Insight
The recent amendments to the guidelines for solar power procurement are crucial for addressing sectoral challenges and enhancing transparency, efficiency, and financial security. By allowing procurers to specify sub-stations for Solar PV Projects, these changes provide clear connectivity options and reduce uncertainty for developers. Stricter compliance with the Capacity Utilization Factor, along with penalties for non-compliance, ensures a consistent energy supply and reliability. Aligning Change in Law provisions with the Electricity Rules facilitates legal conformity and smoother operations. Mandating timely signing of agreements promotes efficiency and swift project initiation and introducing new financial instruments like Insurance Surety Bonds bolsters financial security and mitigates risks for developers and investors. Additionally, emphasizing established technologies minimizes risks and ensures timely project completion. Real-time monitoring with GPS-enabled Weather Stations enhances performance tracking and grid compliance, while cybersecurity measures protect critical infrastructure. These amendments foster a robust, transparent, and efficient procurement process, offering benefits such as improved financial security, technological advancement, and regulatory compliance. APERC Approves Power Purchase Agreement for 4000MU from SECI

Emmvee Invests ₹15,000 Crore in Solar Manufacturing Plant in Bengaluru

The Andhra Pradesh Electricity Regulatory Commission (APERC) approved the purchase of over 4,000 million units of power from Solar Energy Corporation of India (SECI) for the next fiscal. The SECI Power Sale Agreement (PSA) faced controversy over bribery allegations linked to Adani Group. APERC's approval is subject to a pending High Court verdict. The power is meant for agriculture under AP Rural Agriculture Power Limited.

Emmvee Group, a Bengaluru-based Solar Panel maker, will invest ₹15,000 crore in a new manufacturing plant, creating 10,000 jobs. Initially, ₹5,000 crore will be spent to establish a 5GW capacity facility on 120 acres in Bengaluru’s IT Investment Region. This investment is part of Karnataka’s Global Investors Meet 2025. Emmvee produces key Solar components, supporting Sustainable Energy growth. The government will review its land request.

Jakson Plans ₹2,000 Crore Solar Manufacturing Expansion in Khavda, Gujarat

Avaada Plans ₹50,000 Crore Investment in Green Energy

Jakson Engineers Ltd, part of Jakson Group, supplied 100 MW of Solar PV modules to Gujarat Industries Power Co. Ltd (GIPCL) for its 2,375 MW Renewable Energy Park near Khavda, Gujarat. The delivery included 190,000 Helia Plus PERC bifacial panels. Jakson Solar Modules and Cells plans a ₹2,000 crore ($215.9 million) Investment to build a 2.5 GW Solar Cell facility and expand module capacity to 2 GW.

Avaada Group plans to invest Rs 50,000 crore in Madhya Pradesh over five years, with most projects materializing within three to four years, says Chairman Vineet Mittal. Backed by global investors like PTT Group and Brookfield, Avaada has been active in Renewable energy since 2010. Mittal highlighted India's need to expand Solar Capacity to 50 GW annually to meet its 500 GW non-fossil fuel target.

Customs Duty Fails to Protect Indian Solar Industry

Borosil Renewables stated that the imposition of Customs Duty on Solar Glass imports failed to relieve Indian manufacturers due to aggressive price cuts by Chinese competitors. Executive Chairman P K Kheruka highlighted that the firm had to lower its prices to stay competitive. Despite a 14% rise in sales volume, revenue grew only 3.

Analyst Insight
India imposed customs duties on imported solar glass to bolster local manufacturers competitiveness, as the need for this was to create a level playing field for domestic producers against cheaper imports. However, this strategy didn't yield the expected results, primarily because Chinese exporters significantly lowered their prices to counteract the duties. Consequently, Indian manufacturers like Borosil Renewables had to reduce their prices as well, despite facing high production costs. This scenario has strained the financial stability of Indian solar glass manufacturers, escalated costs for solar project developers, and potentially hindered India's renewable energy targets. To tackle these challenges, India needs to enforce anti-dumping measures more rigorously to prevent unfair competition from foreign exporters. Additionally, government subsidies or financial support for domestic manufacturers would help them manage the increased costs. Investing in technological advancements to reduce production expenses is also crucial. MNRE Seeks Comments on Draft MNRE Releases ALMM Solar List

Waaree Energies Secures 410 MWp Solar Module Order from ABREL EPC

The Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE) has released a draft application form for including solar PV cell models in the Approved List of Models and Manufacturers (ALMM). Manufacturers must provide company details, solar cell specifications, calibration certificates, financial records, and ISO certifications. They must also pay fees and submit legal documents. The ALMM listing ensures high-quality solar PV cells for government-backed projects, promoting quality control and domestic manufacturing.

Waaree Energies Ltd received a 410 MWp Solar PV Module order from ABREL EPC Ltd, a subsidiary of Aditya Birla Renewables. Deliveries will begin in FY 2025-26. The company posted a ₹493 crore net profit in Q3, a four-fold increase, with revenue surging 116% to ₹3,457 crore. Waaree holds a 26.5 GW order book worth ₹50,000 crore and has a 12 GW manufacturing capacity.

Indian Railways Signs Agreement with MP for 170 MW Solar Power at ₹2.15/kWh

MP to Develop 800 MW Solar Park Project

Indian Railways signed a 25-year power purchase agreement (PPA) for 170 MW of Solar Power with the Madhya Pradesh government at ₹2.15/kWh. The power will be supplied by Rewa Ultra Mega Solar Power Limited. With 4,260 MW of solar and 3,427 MW of wind capacity, Indian Railways aims for net-zero carbon emissions by 2030. Full electrification by FY26 will ensure operations run on green energy sources.

Madhya Pradesh will develop an 800 MW Solar Park, alternating Energy supply with Uttar Pradesh every six months. The project, with four 200 MW units, will be jointly procured by MP Power Management Company and UP Power Corporation. It aligns with the states' complementary energy demands. The bidding process starts in May, followed by a reverse auction in June and PPA signings in July.

AGEL's AGE24L Commissions 250 MW Solar Project in Jaisalmer, Rajasthan

NTPC REL Issue tender for 600 MW ISTS connected Solar PV Projects in Karnataka

Adani Green Energy Ltd (AGEL) announced that its subsidiary, Adani Green Energy Twenty Four (AGE24L), has operationalized a 250 MW Solar Power Project in Jaisalmer, Rajasthan. With this, AGEL’s total Renewable Capacity now stands at 11,916.1 MW. The decision to commence operations was made at 11:50 a.m. on February 21, 2025, with Power Generation starting on February 22, 2025

NTPC Renewable Energy Limited (NTPC REL) has issued a tender for a 600 MW ISTS-connected Solar Project in Tumkur, Karnataka, with bid submissions open until March 18, 2025. The bidder must arrange government or private land (5 acres/MW) on a freehold/leasehold basis and ensure site preparation, fencing, geotechnical investigations, and lease management for a minimum of 29 years and 11 months.

Coal India, EDF India Partner for Renewable Energy Projects in india

Micromax Expands Into Solar Panel Manufacturing Industry in India

Coal India Limited signed a non-binding agreement with EDF India to form a joint venture for operate and maintain Pumped Storage Project (PSP) and Renewable Energy Projects in India and neighboring countries. EDF India is a subsidiary of Electricite de France SA, an Energy Solutions company. The agreement, announced in a stock exchange filing, marks a step towards expanding Renewable Energy initiatives.

Micromax Informatics is entering the Renewable Energy Sector with its New Venture, Startup Energy, to manufacture high-efficiency Solar Panels in India. Partnering with Chinese firm Jinchen, it will set up a 5GW Solar Module Production line using advanced automation. The move aligns with India's 500GW Renewable Energy target by 2030. Micromax is also diversifying into mobile phone assembly, memory modules, and OTT services.
