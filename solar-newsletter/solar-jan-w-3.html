Waaree Renewable Technologies Sees 85.87% Revenue Increase

Waaree Renewable Technologies Ltd reported strong financial results for Q3 and the nine-months ended Dec. 31, 2024. Revenue for 9M FY 2025 rose 85.87% YoY to INR 1,121.17 crore, with EBITDA up 39.95% to INR 184.57 crore. Net profit increased 43.94% to INR 135.16 crore. Q3 FY 2025 revenue was INR 360.

Analyst Insight
Waaree Renewable Technologies Limited has reported a consolidated revenue of ₹1,121.17 Cr for the nine months ending December 31, 2024, reflecting an 85.87% YoY growth indicating a robust market demand. The company has successfully completed significant solar projects in the EPC segment highlighting the growing need for renewable energy solutions to meet rising energy demands while reducing environmental impact. Despite a 16.7% dip in net profit for Q3 FY25, the company's profit after tax saw a 43.94% increase YoY. Additionally, there is a substantial backlog of projects, totaling 3.4 GWp, to be completed within the next 9-15 months. This includes major new orders, showcasing their ability to secure large scale projects ensuring a robust pipeline for the near future, potentially cushioning any short-term financial dips. Furthermore, their strategic plan to enter the EPC market for Data Centers, both in India and globally represents a significant diversification move. This plan could open new revenue streams and position them as a versatile player in the renewable energy sector. By expanding into the data center industry, they are well positioned to capitalize on the growing demand for energy efficient and sustainable infrastructure, which is crucial for the tech driven economy. This comprehensive strategy, combining robust project execution with strategic market expansion, spots them as a key player in India's green energy transition. Vikram Solar Secures 1GW Solar Module Order

PFC secures 120 billion yen loan from JBIC

Vikram Solar has secured a 1GW order to supply advanced Hypersol N-Type TOPCON Glass-to-Glass solar modules for renewable energy projects in Karnataka, Gujarat, and Rajasthan under JSW Neo Energy Limited. The 580Wp modules will be manufactured in Falta and Oragadam, ensuring high production standards. This order strengthens Vikram Solar’s position in India’s solar market, reflecting its technical expertise and commitment to innovative, sustainable energy solutions.

State-owned PFC has secured a 120 billion Japanese yen loan facility from the Japan Bank for International Cooperation (JBIC), marking the largest green financing deal with an Indian company. Under the agreement, JBIC will provide JPY 72 billion, with the remainder funded by commercial banks. The funds will support PFC’s Renewable Energy portfolio, aiding India's transition to non-fossil-fuel energy. The loan has a long-term tenure of up to 20 years.

TREDA Aims to Install 10,000 Solar Pumps in Rural Areas of Tripura

MERC Approves Additional Wind-Solar Capacity Procurement for MSEDCL

The Tripura Renewable Energy Development Agency (TREDA) aims to install over 10,000 Solar-powered pump sets under the PM Kusum scheme to enhance irrigation in rural areas. So far, 3,616 pumps have been set up, irrigating 7,232 acres for 3,616 farmers. Subsidized pumps are available to farmers, with costs significantly reduced. The scheme will benefit around 21,790 acres, boosting agricultural production without electricity costs for farmers.

The Maharashtra Electricity Regulatory Commission (MERC) approved the adoption of a tariff for 13 MW of additional Wind-Solar Hybrid power from BN Peak Power-1 Pvt. Ltd.(BNPP1PL) under the Green Shoe option. This capacity was part of a larger 2,000 MW procurement process by MSEDCL. MERC rectified an earlier submission error and confirmed the tariff of ₹3.60 per kWh, supporting MSEDCL's renewable energy goals.

KSEBL Signs PSA with Seci for 500 MW Solar Power at tariff Rs. 3.41-3.42/unit

The Kerala State Electricity Regulatory Commission approved a Power Sale Agreement (PSA) between Kerala State Electricity Board Ltd. (KSEBL) and the Solar Energy Corporation of India (SECI) for 500 MW of Solar Power, with 250 MW available for peak hours. The tariff ranges from ₹3.41 to ₹3.42 per unit.

Analyst Insight
KSEBL signed the PSA with SECI to secure 500 MW of solar power including 250 MW for peak-hour supply addressing the rising power demand and fulfilling its RPO. This agreement ensures a stable power supply and supports the integration of renewable energy with storage solutions, benefiting KSEBL by optimizing its energy mix and enhancing operational reliability. The discovered tariff of ₹3.94 to ₹3.95 per unit seems reasonable, given the added interstate transmission charges and losses, but could be considered high due to the low-capacity utilization factor (CUF) of 25-27%. However, advancing the SCOD from September 2026 to June 2026 would reduce transmission charges by 50%, lowering the cost to ₹3.79–₹3.80 per unit. For Indian solar sector, this initiative encourages large-scale renewable energy projects and promotes the growth of energy storage solutions. It also creates opportunities for solar developers, investors, and companies involved in renewable energy infrastructure to invest more fostering a sustainable energy scenario in the country. → Read Full Insight

UPNEDA issues EoI for 2 GW Solar Projects across 8000 acres of Government Land in Uttar Pradesh

Essar Renewables Signs MoU with Maharashtra to invest Rs. 8,000 Cr for 2 GW Renewable Energy for Green Mobility

UPNEDA has issued an Expression of Interest (EoI) for 2 GW Solar Power projects Build-Own Operate basis across four districts of Jalaun, Jhansi, Lalitpur and Chitrakoot in Uttar Pradesh. The projects will utilize 8000 acres of government land, with developers handling all phases, including installation and maintenance. The land will be leased for 30 years. The EoI deadline is February 10, 2025. This initiative aims to boost Renewable Energy capacity, support energy security, and contribute to sustainability.

Essar Renewables Ltd (ERL) has signed an MoU with the Government of Maharashtra at the World Economic Forum in Davos to invest ₹8,000 crore in developing 2 GW of renewable energy for green mobility. The projects, supporting electric vehicle truck charging, will generate 2,000+ jobs and commence in FY 2026-27. This investment aligns with Essar’s goal to surpass 8 GW of renewable capacity in five years.

KERC Stays Grid Support Charges on Captive Projects

The Karnataka Electricity Regulatory Commission (KERC) has stayed grid support charges on captive power projects, including rooftop solar, complying with the Electricity Act, 2003. This decision follows stakeholder concerns that such charges contradict the Act and could deter investment in captive power generation. KERC will examine the issue further, while directing licensees to refrain from imposing the charges until a determination is made. A previous surcharge for open-access consumers was also adjusted.

Analyst Insight
KERC stays grid support charges on captive power projects to ensure the energy security and supply reliability of the power generated. The order aims to clear the concerns of stakeholders regarding the charges as they seem to be inconsistent and discouraging for installation of captive power plants as well as rooftop solar. This decision by KERC supports investment in captive plants and also help improving energy security, ensure supply reliability, and reduce grid dependency. The distribution and transmission licensees are directed to refrain from imposing these charges if the project complies with the provisions which ensures a positive market for developers to encourage investment in such plants which broadly helps increasing RE installed capacity in the country. Advait Energy Wins 40,000 Solar Pump Order from KREDL in Karnataka

US Researchers NREL Explores Efficiency and Manufacturing for Solar Cost Reduction

Gujarat-based Advait Energy Transitions secured an off-grid Solar Order from Karnataka Renewable Energy Development Limited (KREDL) to develop 40,000 Solar Pumps under the PM-KUSUM Component 'B' scheme. The company will design, develop, deploy, and maintain a web portal and mobile app for the project. The execution period is one year, with a five-year operation and maintenance period. The scheme aims to replace diesel pumps with solar solutions in agriculture.

U.S. Department of Energy’s Researchers at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) highlight that increasing the efficiency and manufacturing capacity of metal halide perovskite/silicon Tandem Solar Modules is key to reducing costs. A 2.5% efficiency gain in a module can lower costs as much as doubling factory size. NREL’s cost model emphasizes the importance of scaling production and improving module performance to achieve commercially viable, high-efficiency tandem modules for competitiveness.
