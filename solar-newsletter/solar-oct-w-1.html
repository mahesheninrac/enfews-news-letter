THDCIL Partners with Rajasthan for 1,600 MW PSPs

THDCIL has signed an MoU with the Rajasthan government to establish two 800 MW Pumped Storage Projects (PSPs) at Bisanpura and Rampura Tonk, with an investment of ₹8,800 crore. The projects will use water from the Gudha Dam and Bisalpur reservoirs, enhancing grid stability and promoting sustainable energy solutions. Rajasthan's Chief Minister supports timely project completion.

Analyst Insight
The partnership between THDCL and Rajasthan government aims to strengthen renewable energy infrastructure in the state and increase its RE capacity. With continuous increase in the penetration of RE in the grid, it increases the variability in the supply of power which could result in supply demand gap and also result in power failure. Development of PSP will help support grid stability reducing intermittent nature of RE and providing RTC power supply. The PSP being developed will ensure optimal use of water resource and increase energy efficiency. The PSP being developed will contribute to country’s increase in RE and create opportunity for DISCOMs to procure RE at low cost during demand. India Investigates Chinese Solar Cell Imports for Anti-Dumping Concerns

Alpex Solar to Build 1.6 GW Facility in Mathura

India's Directorate General of Trade Remedies has launched an anti-dumping investigation into Chinese solar cell imports, including modules, following complaints from five domestic companies. The probe aims to assess unfair pricing and its impact on local manufacturers. A decision on potential anti-dumping duties could support India's goal of enhancing domestic solar manufacturing capabilities.

Alpex Solar Ltd, based in Greater Noida, plans to establish a 1.6 GW Solar Cell manufacturing unit in Kosi Kotwan, Mathura, at a cost of Rs 642 crore. The expansion will occur in three phases, with 500 MW operational by October 2025 and completion by September 2026. Funding will come from banks, internal accruals, and equity. The company aims for 1.2 GW solar module capacity by Q3 FY25.

Green Tariffs: Potential Risks for India's Energy Distributors

Uttarakhand Launches Time-of-Day Power Tariff System

India's green electricity initiative may financially strain distribution companies, as highlighted by a CSEP study. Proposed tariffs, set 15% below supply costs, could worsen existing pressures if industrial consumers switch to cheaper options. The report calls for a reassessment of these tariffs to ensure financial stability and equitable access to renewable energy during the transition.

Uttarakhand will become India’s first state to implement time-of-day power tariffs for its 15.87 lakh consumers. Prices will vary across 3 slots, encouraging judicious electricity use. Cheaper rates during the day, linked to Solar Energy production, promote efficient consumption. The initiative aims to benefit consumers and support environmental goals, aligning with India’s Renewable Energy target

Reliance Group Launches 1270MW Renewable Projects in Bhutan

Reliance Group, led by Anil Ambani, is entering Bhutan to establish 1,270 MW of Solar and Hydroelectric Projects. In partnership with Druk Holding and Investments, the initiative aims to enhance Bhutan's Renewable Energy landscape and support net-zero goals. Key projects include a 500 MW solar Plant and the 770 MW Chamkharchhu-1 hydro project, marking significant foreign investment in Bhutan.

Analyst Insight
Reliance Group, led by Anil Ambani, is entering Bhutan with plans to develop 1,270 MW of renewable energy projects, including a 500 MW solar plant and the 770 MW Chamkharchhu-1 hydro project. In partnership with Druk Holding and Investments, this initiative will significantly benefit India by facilitating cross-border energy trading. By accessing Bhutan’s abundant clean energy resources, India can reduce its reliance on fossil fuels and lower its carbon footprint, contributing to its own sustainability goals. Furthermore, Bhutan’s favorable policies and strong renewable energy infrastructure position it as a key partner in India’s transition to greener energy sources. This collaboration not only enhances energy security for India but also supports Bhutan’s economic growth through increased revenue from energy exports. Goldi Solar Plans Major Expansion in Manufacturing Capacity in Gujarat

India's Matrix Gas Plans ₹10-12 Billion IPO for Green Projects

Goldi Solar plans to expand its module manufacturing capacity from 3 GW to 14 GW by FY26 and achieve 4 GW in Solar Cell production by FY27 at a new facility in Kosamba, Gujarat. Utilizing advanced AI technologies, the company reported a turnover increase from Rs 600 crore in FY22 to Rs 1,750 crore in FY24, with plans to hire over 4,000 employees and unlock new market opportunities across India.

India's Matrix Gas & Renewables plans to raise ₹10-12 billion ($119-$143 million) through an IPO to fund its green hydrogen projects, targeting 1,000 megawatts in three years. CEO Chirag Kotecha announced plans to file for the IPO next year, capitalizing on India's booming equity market. The company seeks to finance ₹35 billion in capital expenditures with 70% debt and 30% equity.

Avaada Invests INR 13,650 Crore in Renewable Energy

Avaada Group Launches Integrated 20,000 MW Solar Plant in Nagpur

Avaada Group has initiated construction of its new manufacturing facility, Avaada Electro, in Nagpur, Maharashtra, as part of an INR 13,650 crore investment. The facility will produce solar components and next-gen batteries. The first phase targets a capacity of 5 GW for solar cells. The project aims to create over 5,000 jobs, emphasizing skill development and diversity.

A Bhoomi Pujan for an integrated Solar plant by Avaada Group was held in Nagpur, with production expected to start by April 2025. Maharashtra's Deputy CM, Devendra Fadnavis, announced plans for 20,000 MW of solar power, emphasizing the plant's advanced technology. Additionally, an MoU was signed for two Pumped Storage Projects totaling 2750 MW, enhancing Maharashtra's energy sustainability.

India's Solar Capacity to Hit 107 GW by 2030

India's clean energy capacity is expected to grow significantly by 2030, aiming for self-sufficiency in solar and wind energy. Projected capacities include 107 GW of solar PV, 20 GW of wind, and 69 GWh of battery cells. Policies like the Production-Linked Incentive scheme support domestic manufacturing, while challenges in underutilized wind capacity and the hydrogen sector.

Analyst Insight
India's clean energy capacity is expected to grow significantly by 2030 due to robust government policies like the Production-Linked Incentive (PLI) scheme, which encourages domestic manufacturing of solar PV modules, wind nacelles, and battery cells. This growth is projected to enhance self-sufficiency in solar and wind energy, contributing to reduced reliance on imports and fostering a competitive clean energy market. Achieving this capacity will involve bolstering local production capabilities, addressing technological hurdles, and developing a comprehensive battery supply chain ecosystem. As a result, India stands to gain substantial economic benefits, including increased exports—evidenced by the rise in solar module shipments to the US—and the potential to position itself as a key player in the global clean energy supply chain, while also addressing challenges in sectors like offshore wind and hydrogen. → Read Full Insight

NHPC Announces Tender for 1200 MW ISTS-Connected Solar Projects in India

UPPCL Seeks Approval for 1000 MW Solar Power

NHPC Limited has launched a tender to select Solar Power Generators for 1200 MW of ISTS-connected Solar Projects in India, with an additional 1200 MW available via a Green Shoe Option. Bidding runs from October 8 to November 4, 2024, with the bid opening on November 8. Winning bidders will sign a 25-year Power Purchase Agreement, promoting established solar technologies.

The Uttar Pradesh Power Corporation Limited (UPPCL) seeks approval to procure 1,000 MW of solar power via competitive bidding to meet rising demand and renewable obligations. The Central Electricity Regulatory Commission approved tariffs of ₹2.52-₹2.53 per kWh while allowing flexible trading margins capped at ₹0.07. Consumer interests will be prioritized in future negotiations.

India's Solar Sector Surges Past 25 GW Capacity

MNRE: Operational Guidelines for Component of Service Charge un PM Surya Ghar

FY24-25 marks a pivotal year for India’s Solar Sector, with capacity additions expected to exceed 25GW for the first time, largely driven by domestic suppliers amid reduced Chinese imports. Premier Energies has made headlines by becoming the second Indian solar cell maker to list publicly. India’s cell manufacturing landscape is rapidly evolving, with the increased focus on domestic production.

The MNRE issued operational guidelines for the PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana, which aims to install Rooftop Solar Plants in one crore households with a budget of ₹75,021 crore. The guidelines detail the implementation of service charges, including allocations for agencies managing the scheme. The initiative promotes solar energy adoption across India until 2026-27.

Jakson Engineers Invests Rs 20 Billion in 2.5 GW Solar Manufacturing

MERC drafts 2024 regulations on demand flexibility and management

Jakson Engineers Ltd (JEL) will invest Rs 20 billion to establish a 2,500 MW Solar Cell manufacturing facility, developed in two phases, with the first phase due in 15 months. Additionally, JEL is expanding its solar module production to 2,000 MW, creating 1,200 jobs. This initiative aims to enhance domestic production, support India’s renewable energy goals, and achieve Net Zero emissions.

MERC's draft regulations update demand-side management (DSM) by introducing demand flexibility to better align energy consumption with Renewable Energy supply. The combined efforts of the distribution licensees resulted in energy savings of about 50 MW. The framework sets flexibility targets, encourages technologies like EV charging and thermal storage, and offers incentives for exceeding goals.
