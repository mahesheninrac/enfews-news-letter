Nepal Opens Tender for 800 MW Solar Projects

Nepal Electricity Authority (NEA) seeks proposals for 800 MW grid-connected solar projects. Projects must connect to specified substations, with sizes starting from 1 MW for DCS and 10 MW for grid connections. Bidders can apply for up to 150 MW total. Deadline: July 9. Nepal had 115 MW solar capacity by 2023 (IRENA).

Analyst Insight
This tender shows Nepal’s interest to diversify its energy mix, currently dominated by hydropower, which is susceptible to seasonal variations and the impacts of climate change, such as altered rainfall patterns and reduced snowmelt. Though Nepal has achieved self sufficiency in generating electricity, it has to import electricity from India during winters.→ Read Full Insight

PM Surya Ghar Scheme: KERC's Subsidized Solar

KERC's latest order proposes FY25 tariffs for Karnataka's grid-connected solar plants. MW-scale projects set at Rs3.04/unit, with BESS at Rs5.66/unit. Rooftop solar tariffs vary: 1 KW up to sanctioned load at Rs .20/unit.

Analyst Insight
Consumers and Distributers in Karnataka seeking or providing electricity to the grid could be affected as KERC has revised its tariffs for FY25 effective from April 1,2024 for grid-connected solar plants.Revised Tariffs creates opportunities for capex investments in Karnataka because of the low tariffs set by the Karnataka Government.
