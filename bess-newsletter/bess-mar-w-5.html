TGGENCO’s Auction gets completed at the rate Rs 2.40 to Rs 2.45 per KWh

China Publicize procurement of 5 types of sodium ion Batteries

Telangana Power Generation Corporation Limited (TGGENCO’s) has completed the auction for a 250MW/500MWh standalone BESS in Telangana. The auction resulted in a competitive tariff, setting a benchmark with a discovered rate of ₹2.403 per kWh. The tariffs in the auction ranged from ₹2.40 to ₹2.45 per kWh, reflecting strong developer interest and competitiveness in India’s renewable energy sector. Three companies secured contracts for a total capacity of 225 MW. Pace Digitek won the largest share, securing 125 MW at ₹2.45153 per kWh.

A battery Storage project integrating five different Technologies is taking shape in a suburban district of South Shanghai China. Once completed, Fengxian Xinghou Comprehensive Multiple New Energy Technological Route Comparison Test Shows Phase I will feature 40MW/160MWh which will spread across two hectares. The project will feature 10 MW/40 MWh of lithium-ion batteries, 10 MW/40 MWh zinc-iron redox flow batteries, 10 MW/40 MWh all-vanadium redox flow batteries, 5 MW/20 MWh of sodium-ion batteries, and 5 MW/20 MWh semi-solid-state batteries.

MERC approves MSEDCL’s 250MW/500MWh standalone BESS

Fluence uses Mosaic software to co-optimize Gentari Renewables for 172MW/408MWh BESS

The Maharashtra Electricity Regulatory Commission has approved the Maharashtra State Electricity Distribution Co. Ltd 250MW/500MWh BESS with additional green shoe capacity of 500MW/1000MWh. MSEDCL floated the tender on August 16, 2024, for battery storage projects at its substations, following guidelines from the Ministry of Power. A total of 14 bidders participated in the process, with Pace Digitek Private Limited emerging as the lowest bidder at ₹2,19,001 per MW per month. The discovered tariff was significantly lower than recent BESS tenders conducted by other utilities like SECI and GUVNL, making the project cost-effective.

BC Jindal Group gets LoA of securing 300MW solar+ storage project

Equis starts construction of 500MWh BESS with close of debt financing in Australia

BC Jindal Group gets LoA for the 300MW solar+ storage project from NHPC. This project is part of NHPC’s 1200 MW inter-state transmission system (ISTS)-connected solar power initiative, which includes 600 MW/1200 MW energy storage systems. Earlier, JIRE won 180 MW but with the green shoe option the company won another 120MW making a total project capacity 300MW. As part of the contract, JIRE is required to install an energy storage system (ESS) with at least 0.5 MW/1 MWh capacity for every 1 MW contracted capacity. This ensures reliable energy supply and grid stability.

The Singaporean Infrastructure developer Equis has reached to the Financial close of Calala BESS and begin its construction. It has borrowed AUD 260 million of non-recourse senior debt from Australian lender Westpac, France’s Societe Generale & Japan-Based Sumitomo Banking Corporation. The project will be structured as two facilities, one of 100 MW/200 MWh and another of 150 MW/300 MWh. An energy offtake agreement has been signed for the smaller of the two sections. Meanwhile, the 150 MW portion will operate on a merchant basis, marking the first merchant BESS debt financing in New South Wales.
