Lower Battery Storage System Costs Could Limit New Coal Plants

India may limit new coal plants by 2032 if battery storage system (BESS) costs fall 15% annually, according to a report. Currently, coal generates nearly 75% of India's power. As solar and wind contribute more, improved storage could meet 83% of daytime demand by 2032. Significant BESS cost reductions...

Analyst Insight
To reduce reliance on coal and increase the electricity demand met by renewable energy, India needs to bring down the cost of BESS to nearly half of its current price so that it could act as an alternative to coal power plants generating electricity at comparable or lower prices.
