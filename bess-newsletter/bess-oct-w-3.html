MERC Approves Energy Storage for MSEDCL's 1468 MW FDRE Procurement

MERC approved MSEDCL's procurement of 1,468 MW of FDRE from SJVN, incorporating Energy Storage Systems. This 25-year contract, with tariffs of ₹4.45 and ₹4.46/kWh, aims to fulfill MSEDCL's Renewable Purchase Obligation (RPO). The Energy Storage will ensure firm power availability during peak hours, providing four hours of daily supply with 90% reliability.

Prozeal Green Energy Wins SECI BESS Tender

South Africa Launches 180MW/720MWh Battery Storage Projects

Ahmedabad-based Prozeal Green Energy has won a SECI tender for a 25 MW (AC) Solar PV Power Plant with a 20 MW/50 MWh Battery Storage System in Leh, totaling INR 313.12 crore. The project, crucial for regional energy security, will use approved Solar Modules and includes design, construction, and maintenance services. Prozeal has over 2 GW installed capacity.

South Africa's Ministry of Electricity and Energy announced the commercial close of two Battery Storage Projects, totaling 180 MW/720 MWh. The projects include Mulilo Renewable Development with EDF International and Scatec ASA's Mogobe BESS, set to begin construction and expected to operate by September 2026. The total investment amounts to ZAR 5.3 billion (USD 299m/EUR 275m).

India’s BESS Tender Results Signal Renewable Energy Disruption

IndiGrid, Kintech & HG Infra Win NVVN's 500MW Battery Storage Auction

Recent BESS tender results in India indicate a potential surge in energy storage capacity, driven by falling lithium cell prices. India aims for 41,650 MW by 2029-30, with significant cost reductions leading to aggressive bidding. The shift to a BOO model enhances developer confidence. However, risks remain, especially concerning domestic capacity and potential tariffs on imports.

IndiGrid 2 Limited, Kintech Synergy, and H.G. Infra Engineering won contracts in NVVN's first standalone Battery Energy Storage Auction, totaling 500 MW/1000 MWh. Tariffs ranged from ₹2.37 to ₹2.38 lakhs/MW/month, significantly lower than previous rates. The auction, launched in September 2024, is part of a larger 4,000 MWh capacity initiative supported by Viability Gap Funding.

GUVNL Launches 200MW/1600MWh Battery Energy Storage System in Gujarat

GUVNL has launched a Request for Selection for a 200MW/1600MWh Battery Energy Storage System project in Gujarat, operating under a Build-Own-Operate model. Bids will be submitted in a two-envelope process, focusing on technical and financial aspects. Developers must meet performance criteria and connect to the local grid, with penalties for delays and shortfalls.

Australia Targets 4GW Dispatchable Power and 6GW Renewables

Scatec Secures Financing for 103MW/412MWh BESS in South Africa

Australia’s Energy Minister Chris Bowen announced the government is seeking 4GW of Dispatchable Power and 6GW of Renewable Energy in the upcoming Capital Investment Scheme (CIS) tender. This new round, launching mid-November, aims to enhance energy reliability as coal phases out. The CIS is designed to attract investments while maintaining market incentives for technology and location choices.

Scatec has achieved financial close for the 103MW/412MWh Mogobe Battery Energy Storage System in South Africa, the continent's largest standalone BESS project. With a total investment of R3 billion, Scatec will hold 51% equity. The facility, aimed at enhancing grid stability, is backed by a 15 Year PPA. Scatec's CEO emphasizes this milestone for renewable energy in South Africa.

Oriana Power Plans Rs 10,000 Crore Investment in Solar, BESS, Hydrogen in India

Eco Stor Plans 300MW/716MWh Battery Storage in Germany

Oriana Power plans to invest Rs 10,000 crore in Solar, Green Hydrogen, and Battery Energy Storage over the next three to four years. The company targets 1 GW in EPC and 400 MW in IPP by 2026. With an order book of Rs 2,500 crore and a 900 MW Project pipeline, they aim for significant growth, having executed over 200 MW across India.

Eco Stor GmbH is set to construct a 300MW/716MWh Battery Energy Storage System in Trossingen, Germany, starting in 2027 and expected to be operational in 2028. This follows their plans for a 103.5 MW facility in Schuby and additional projects nationwide, totaling over 2 GWh in storage capacity since the company's founding in 2021.
