MNRE: Amendment to Onshore Wind Power Project Guidelines

The guidelines for onshore wind power projects emphasize micrositing, optimizing turbine placement for maximum energy production while adhering to safety and environmental standards. Developers must use wind flow modeling and comply with specified distances from public infrastructure and residential areas, facilitating efficient land use and potential for future upgrades and multi-cropping.

Analyst Insight
Amendment to Onshore Wind Power Project Guidelines aims to enhance wind power project development by emphasizing optimization and flexibility in turbine placement. This could positively impact existing wind farms by improving energy production and land use. Existing wind farms can reassess their turbine placement using the new guidelines. Repowering (upgrading older turbines) could benefit from optimized micrositing. Developers may explore better locations within the farm to maximize energy output. Wind Power Hits 33-Month Low in U.S

DOE Allocates $48.6 Million for Offshore Wind R&D

Wind Power Production in the U.S. hit a 33-month low, forcing increased reliance on Natural Gas-Fired Plants to meet Energy Demands amidst soaring summer temperatures. Despite heavy investment in renewables like Wind and Solar, fluctuations in Wind availability underscore the ongoing need for gas-fired backup to ensure Power reliability, especially during Peak Demand periods.

The DOE's Wind Energy Technologies Office is investing $48.6 million in Offshore Wind Projects to advance Floating Platforms, fixed-bottom foundations, and wildlife protection technologies. This funding aims to boost the domestic supply chain, enhance academic research, and protect infrastructure from lightning, promoting a clean energy economy and a carbon-free Power Sector.
