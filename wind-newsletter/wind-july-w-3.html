MNRE: Amendment to Onshore Wind Power Project Guidelines

The guidelines for onshore wind power projects emphasize micrositing, optimizing turbine placement for maximum energy production while adhering to safety and environmental standards. Developers must use wind flow modeling and comply with specified distances from public infrastructure and residential areas, facilitating efficient land use and potential for future upgrades and multi-cropping.

Analyst Insight
Amendment to Onshore Wind Power Project Guidelines aims to enhance wind power project development by emphasizing optimization and flexibility in turbine placement. This could positively impact existing wind farms by improving energy production and land use. Existing wind farms can reassess their turbine placement using the new guidelines. Repowering (upgrading older turbines) could benefit from optimized micrositing. Developers may explore better locations within the farm to maximize energy output. California Approves $10bn Climate Bond for Port Upgrades

Rezolv Energy's 10-Year VPPA: 461MW Wind Power

California approved a $10bn climate bond, with $475m allocated for port upgrades to support floating wind. This investment aims to achieve California's offshore wind targets of 5GW by 2030 and 25GW by 2045. The state's strategic plan includes a multi-port strategy and estimates $11bn-$12bn for port infrastructure upgrades to advance offshore wind development.

Rezolv Energy signed a 10-year VPPA with Bekaert in Romania, involving its 461MW Vifor wind farm. Bekaert will purchase 100GWh of renewable power annually. The project, developed by Rezolv and Low Carbon in Buzau County, will make Vifor one of Europe’s largest onshore wind farms. Bekaert aims to enhance its renewable energy portfolio, promoting sustainability and greener operations.

C1 Wedge Technology Revolutionizes Offshore Wind Infrastructure

France Advances Offshore Wind with $11.6 billion Funding

A consortium of industry leaders, including bp, TotalEnergies & Orsted, launched a Joint Industry Project (JIP) to advance the C1 Wedge Connection technology in offshore wind. Developed to replace traditional bolted flanges, it promises higher capacity, faster installation, & enhanced safety.

France has secured €10.8 billion from the European Commission to build two offshore wind farms, accelerating its energy transition and reducing reliance on Russian fuels. The South Atlantic farm will generate 1,000-1,200 MW, producing 3.9 TWh annually, while the Normandy farm, at 1,400-1,600 MW, will generate 6.1 TWh yearly. This funding aims to reach 18 GW of offshore wind capacity by 2035.

Technip Energies, SBM Offshore Launch Floating Offshore Wind Joint Venture

Vestas Secures 2.8 GW in Q2 Orders Across 13 Countries

Technip Energies and SBM Offshore have launched Ekwil, a 50/50 joint venture specializing in floating offshore wind solutions. Based in France, Ekwil merges Technip Energies' Semi-submersible INO and SBM Offshore's Tension Leg Platform Float4Wind technologies. Led by industry veterans Séverine Baudic and Willy Gauttier, Ekwil aims to advance RE with integrated innovation and delivery capabilities.

In Q2, Vestas, a leading OEM, secured 19 orders across 13 countries, totaling 2.8 GW. Highlights include a 660 MW order for V236-15.0 MW turbines for Germany's Nordseecluster A and a 577 MW EPC order in Australia for V162-6.2 MW turbines. Vestas expands beyond traditional markets like Europe and the US, emphasizing growth in global wind energy sectors.

Invenergy Plans $621M Wind Farm in South Dakota

England Lifts Ban: Onshore Wind Development Now Allowed

Invenergy, based in Chicago, plans to build a wind farm near Brandt, SD, through its subsidiary, Deuel Harvest Wind Energy South. This $621 million project would feature 68 turbines spread over 54 sq mi of private land. Expected to generate 250 MW, it adds to SD's 3,462 MW wind capacity, potentially creating 243 construction jobs and yielding $78 million in landowner payments over 30 years.

The UK government has lifted the ban on onshore wind in England to double capacity by 2030. Currently, the UK has 15GW of onshore wind capacity, mainly in Scotland. A key step towards net zero, the change removes previous planning obstacles and aligns onshore wind with other energy development. Further proposals aim to support renewable energy and empower local communities in decision-making.
