MNRE: Amendment to Onshore Wind Power Project Guidelines

The guidelines for onshore wind power projects emphasize micrositing, optimizing turbine placement for maximum energy production while adhering to safety and environmental standards. Developers must use wind flow modeling and comply with specified distances from public infrastructure and residential areas, facilitating efficient land use and potential for future upgrades and multi-cropping.

Analyst Insight
Amendment to Onshore Wind Power Project Guidelines aims to enhance wind power project development by emphasizing optimization and flexibility in turbine placement. This could positively impact existing wind farms by improving energy production and land use. Existing wind farms can reassess their turbine placement using the new guidelines. Repowering (upgrading older turbines) could benefit from optimized micrositing. Developers may explore better locations within the farm to maximize energy output. If there's available land within the wind farm, intercropping (combining wind power with other land uses) becomes more feasible. → Read Full Insight
