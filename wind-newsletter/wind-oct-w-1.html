SECI Completes 600 MW Wind Solar Hybrid Auction

SECI's recent auction for 600 MW Wind Solar Hybrid Power Projects marks a significant milestone in India's Renewable Energy sector. Major companies competed fiercely, with ACME Solar and Juniper Green Energy winning 300 MW and 150 MW at ₹3.25 per kWh, while Sembcorp secured 150 MW at ₹3.26. These results reflect declining tariffs and strong competition, supporting India's Renewable Energy goals.

Analyst Insight
The auction completed by SECI for development of hybrid RE project is a step towards achieving the country’s goal of increasing RE capacity addition and reducing reliance on fossil fuels. The tariffs discovered through this auction represents strong market competition and interest from key players in the hybrid projects and with more investment expected in the upcoming years, the tariff is expected to go further down making renewable energy more affordable and attractive in India. Lower tariff rates will help DISCOMs and other utility to procure RE during peak hours at lower cost to meet their electricity demand. With increase in hybrid projects seen across the country, there is opportunity for local manufacturers of RE components and OEMs to meet the demand of country and even export their products. Wind Power Generation Declines in Tamil Nadu This Year

CERC: SECI Petition for Tariff Adoption of 1,175 MW Wind Projects

The southwest monsoon's withdrawal marked the end of the high wind season, with Tamil Nadu's Wind Power Generation down 9.06% in 2024 compared to 2023. While early Generation was promising, erratic wind patterns and rainfall in August led to a significant decline. Despite this year's disappointing season, industry experts remain optimistic about future investments.

The Solar Energy Corporation of India Limited (SECI) filed a petition for tariff approval for 1,175 MW of Wind Power projects across Gujarat, Karnataka, and Rajasthan. These projects, awarded to companies like JSW Neo Energy and Torrent Power, followed a competitive bidding process. The regulatory commission approved the tariffs and a trading margin for UPPCL's power purchase agreements.

AM Green and GAIL Collaborate on 2.5 GW Renewable Energy Projects in India

India's 122 GW Wind Capacity Goal for Energy Security

AM Green BV and GAIL (India) Limited have partnered to develop Solar and Wind hybrid Renewable Energy Projects up to 2.5 GW across India. Their collaboration includes supplying 350 KTA of CO2 for eMethanol production, promoting sustainability and decarbonization. The initiative aims to support India’s green energy goals and enhance the Production of Green Ammonia and biofuels.

India must ramp up Wind Energy deployment by 22% annually to achieve a target of 122 GW by FY 2032. With 21 states planning over 100 GW by 2030, even regions with limited wind potential are expected to import surplus power. Wind energy is essential for meeting non-solar hour demand, reducing reliance on thermal power, and enhancing energy security.

GAIL and AM Green Partner for 2.5 GW Solar-Wind Hybrid Projects in India

GAIL India Limited and AM Green have signed a memorandum of understanding to develop Solar-Wind hybrid Renewable Energy projects in India, with a capacity of up to 2.5 GW. The agreement includes a study on utilizing 350 kilotonnes of CO2 from GAIL’s gas processing plants to produce eMethanol from Renewable Energy, supported by Energy Storage Systems for continuous power supply.

Analyst Insight
The MoU signed between GAIL and AM Green Partner aims to explore sustainable energy solutions by setting up hybrid projects integrated with PSP and processing of CO2 to generate eMethanol. This MoU will help increase RE capacity in the country and also support for round the clock supply of power by developing and integrating pump storage projects with hybrid projects which will result in uninterrupted power supply to the end users. This partnership helps reducing CO2 emissions as the CO2 from GAIL’s gas processing plants will be used to produce eMethanol which can be used as a fuel for vehicles, in fuel cells, for electricity generation offering hard-to-abate sectors like shipping a way to reach their sustainability goals. This brings solution to effectively utilize CO2 generated by GAIL and such methods provide effective solution for reusing CO2 which is the need of hour to help India transition towards greener energy and this could result in more such partnership between players in RE sector. IEA Warns of Wind Turbine Supply Chain Bottlenecks by 2030

Avaada Group Invests ₹1,00,000 Crore in Rajasthan’s Renewable Energy

The IEA stresses the urgent need for increased investment in Wind Turbine manufacturing to prevent supply chain issues by 2030, especially for Offshore Wind. Despite growth in onshore installations, global capacity may barely meet demand. The report highlights the importance of flexible energy solutions and forecasts significant increases in wind energy, particularly in China, Europe, and the U.S.

Avaada Group plans to invest ₹1,00,000 crore in Rajasthan, enhancing the state's Renewable Energy capabilities. Key projects include a 1.2 GW Pumped Hydro Storage, 1 MTPA Green Ammonia, 1 GW Wind, and 10 GW Solar initiatives. This investment will create jobs and promote green manufacturing, positioning Rajasthan as a Green Energy hub and major contributor to India's sustainability goals by 2030.

Adani & Google collaboration for New Solar-Wind Hybrid Project in Gujarat

Romania Unveils Roadmap for 7 GW Offshore Wind Energy Development

Adani Group will provide clean energy for Google’s cloud services in India through a new Solar-Wind hybrid project at its 30 GW Khavda renewable energy park in Gujarat. The project is expected to start operations in Q3 2025. Google aims to fully transition its cloud services to clean energy by 2030 and plans to establish clean energy-powered data centers.

The Romanian government, with support from the European Commission and World Bank, has launched a roadmap to develop Offshore Wind Energy. Targeting up to 7 GW capacity in the Black Sea, it aims to enhance energy security, drive economic growth, and create jobs. The initiative aligns with EU Green Deal goals and emphasizes international collaboration for success.

Tata Power Bids for 3 GW Wind Turbines for Wind-Solar Hybrid Projects

Tata Power is inviting bids for over 3 GW of Wind Turbines to enhance its Wind-Solar hybrid projects, potentially worth over Rs 21,000 crore. This procurement could rise to 5 GW, totaling Rs 35,000 crore. The company aims for 1 GW of annual Wind capacity, targeting 100% clean energy by FY45. Currently, it has 10.5 GW of Renewable Capacity and plans substantial investments in the sector.

Analyst Insight
Tata Power is inviting bids for supply of wind turbines to meet the requirements of its wind and hybrid projects as the company aims to get 20 GW of RE capacity by 2030 with a target of setting 1 GW of wind capacity per year. These bids could prove to be a turning point for wind energy sector as the wind power project demand in the country has been relatively slow in the recent years. The large-scale order will intensify competition among the wind OEMs and turbine suppliers leading to a healthy market response as a result of which, the price of these turbine may decrease which will present wind energy as an affordable solution. These RE projects will supply power to the groups company which will help TATA transition towards green energy broadly aligning with country’s goal of reducing carbon footprints and adopt RE solutions. Adani and Google Partner for Clean Energy Project in India

Vestas Signs Agreement for 1.1 GW Inch Cape Wind Project in Scotland

The Adani Group and Google have announced a collaboration to enhance sustainability and boost clean energy in India. Adani will supply energy from a solar-wind hybrid project at Khavda, set to start in 2025. This partnership aims to support Google’s 24/7 carbon-free energy goal, aligning with both companies' commitment to decarbonization and renewable solutions.

Vestas has entered a conditional agreement with Inch Cape Offshore Limited for the 1.1 GW Inch Cape project in Scotland, involving the supply and installation of 72 V236-15.0 MW wind turbines. This initiative aims to enhance the UK's renewable energy landscape, expected to generate over 5 TWh annually. Installation is set to start in 2026, with completion anticipated in 2027.

RWE and TotalEnergies Launch 4 GW Offshore Wind Projects in Germany

Nordex Secures 500MW Wind Turbine Orders in Canada

RWE and TotalEnergies are partnering to develop two Offshore Wind Projects off Germany's coast, totaling 4 GW capacity. TotalEnergies will acquire a 50% stake in both projects, awarded during a recent auction. With construction starting in 2029 and 2030, the collaboration aims to enhance offshore wind energy, contributing to Germany's energy transition and decarbonization efforts.

Nordex has received orders for 500MW of Wind Turbines in Canada, consisting of 74 N163 machines with 15 to 30-year service agreements. Scheduled for delivery in 2025 and 2026, the turbines will feature the Advanced Anti-Icing System for winter performance. Nordex aims to enhance efficiency for Canadian customers, building on its extensive experience in cold climates.
