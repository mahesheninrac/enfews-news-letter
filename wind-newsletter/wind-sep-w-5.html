Gujarat Achieves 14.7 GW Solar Capacity, Pursues 31.9 GW Hybrid

Gujarat leads India's Solar Energy initiative with an installed capacity of 14.7GW, making it the second-largest in the country. The state aims to add 32 GW of Solar-Wind Hybrid Projects, contributing to India's goal of 500GW of non-fossil energy by 2030. Innovative Solar Parks and Green Hydrogen Plans further enhance Gujarat's Renewable Energy landscape, promoting sustainability & reducing costs.

Analyst Insight
Gujarat has achieved an impressive solar capacity of 14.7 GW and is now pursuing the development of 31.9 GW of solar-wind hybrid projects. This ambitious plan not only enhances energy security but also reduces greenhouse gas emissions, aligning with global climate commitments. By investing in hybrid projects that combine solar and wind energy, the state aims to maximize resource efficiency and ensure a more stable energy supply. Additionally, the development of innovative solar parks will facilitate large-scale energy generation, making it easier to distribute electricity across the grid. This initiative in Gujarat presents several opportunities for various stakeholders. For businesses, there is a chance to invest in and collaborate on renewable energy projects, especially in solar parks and hybrid systems. For researchers and innovators, the focus on green hydrogen technology opens avenues for developing sustainable energy storage solutions. Windergy India 2024: Advancing the Wind Energy Sector

NLC India Aims To Add 10 GW Renewable Energy By 2030

The sixth edition of Windergy India will take place from October 23-25, 2024, at the Chennai Trade Centre, uniting industry leaders, policymakers, and innovators in the wind energy sector. With around 300 exhibitors and a focus on advancements in technology and policy, the event aims to drive India’s renewable energy goals and promote international collaboration in sustainable practices.

NLC India Ltd (NLCIL) has produced 1,234 crore units of Green Power, preventing one crore tonne of CO2 emissions. The company aims for 10,000 MW of Renewable Energy capacity by 2030, currently operating 1,380 MW of Solar and 51 MW of Wind. New subsidiaries have been formed to enhance clean energy initiatives, with significant projects underway across several states.

Odisha Approves 750MW Renewable Energy Projects Worth Rs. 4,671.81 Crore

India’s Wind Turbine Market: Envision Leads with 41%

The Single Window Committee (SWC) of Odisha's Energy Department has approved Renewable Energy Projects worth Rs. 4,671.81 crore, totaling 750 MW capacity. Key projects include a 600 MW Pumped Storage Plant in Kalahandi and three Wind projects totaling 148.5 MW. These initiatives aim to help Odisha achieve its goal of 10 GW Renewable Energy by 2030.

A MEX Intelligence report highlights the Indian wind turbine market, where Envision leads with a 41% share and 7.7 GW total orders, including 1.6 GW in Q2 2024. Suzlon follows at 20% with 3.9 GW, while Inox Wind holds 17% with 3.2 GW. European players are declining, and the market anticipates a shift to larger turbines by 2025-26, amid growing demand for hybrid projects.

Tata Power Signs ₹1.2 Lakh Crore MoU with Rajasthan

Tata Power has signed a significant ₹1.2 lakh crore MoU with the Government of Rajasthan, aimed at transforming the state into a power surplus region over ten years. The plan focuses on Renewable Energy, including 10,000 MW capacity, manufacturing, and infrastructure upgrades. This initiative will create over 28,000 jobs and position Rajasthan as a key player in India's clean energy transition.

Analyst Insight
The recent ₹1.2 lakh crore MoU between Tata Power and the Government of Rajasthan is a strategic response to India’s growing energy demands and commitment to renewable sources. As Rajasthan already boasts over 30,000 MW of renewable energy capacity, this partnership aims to leverage the state’s vast solar and wind potential, contributing to the national goal of 500 GW by 2030. While the state faces challenges such as infrastructure gaps and the need for further investment, the agreement promises to add 10,000 MW of renewable capacity, enhancing energy security and potentially achieving power surplus status. This transition will reduce fossil fuel dependence and lower energy costs, while also creating local employment and boosting skill development. By improving infrastructure and attracting investments, Rajasthan can solidify its status as a renewable energy hub. MNRE Amends Offshore Wind Energy Lease Regulations Significantly

NTPC Green Energy Partners with Rajasthan for 25 GW Renewable Projects

MNRE amended Offshore Wind Energy lease rules, allowing leases for up to 35 years, extendable based on viability & safety. The updated definition clarifies leasing rights over seabeds within territorial waters and the Exclusive Economic Zone. New regulations streamline the leasing process, requiring approvals from various Govt. entities and restricting the transfer of lease rights without consent.

NTPC Green Energy Ltd has signed a MoU with the Rajasthan government to develop 25 gigawatts of Renewable Energy Projects. The agreement was finalized during the Rising Rajasthan Investor Meet in New Delhi on September 30, 2024. The MoU was exchanged between NGEL’s Director K S Sundaram and Alok ACS (Energy), with Rajasthan's Chief Minister and senior officials in attendance.

Divento Launches 3GW Floating Offshore Wind Projects in Italy

GE Vernova Signs Turbine Supply Agreement for Australian Wind Farm

GreenIT, a JV of Plenitude, CDP Equity, & Copenhagen Infrastructure Partners, launched Divento to develop a 3GW portfolio of floating offshore wind projects in Italy. These plants, located off Sicily, Latium, & Sardinia, will produce 8TWh of renewable energy for 2.5M households, supporting Italy’s 2030 decarbonization goals and securing the first large-scale EIA approval in the Mediterranean.

GE Vernova has partnered with Aula Energy and CS Energy to supply 38 turbines for the Boulder Creek Wind Farm in Queensland, Australia. This 228 MW project, located 40 kilometers south-west of Rockhampton, will begin site preparations by late 2024, with operations expected to start in 2027. The agreement includes a five-year full-service contract for maintenance.

Bowen Approves Feasibility Licence for 1.2GW Spinifex Wind Project

Stanwell acquires 436.5 MW Queensland Wind project

Australia's Minister for Climate Change and Energy, Chris Bowen, has provisionally approved a feasibility licence for the 1.2GW Spinifex Offshore Wind Project near Victoria. The project, developed by Alinta Energy and Parkwind, will proceed to detailed consultations and environmental studies to assess its viability, aiming to create jobs and support renewable energy growth in the region.

Stanwell Corp is closing a deal for 436.5 MW Tarong West Wind Farm project from RES in the north eastern Aussie state. The project will be made up of 97 turbines and will receive a state funding of $ 538 m. It is expected to generate electricity equivalent to consumption of 230,000 homes. Stanwell has set a goal of achieving 9 GW to 10 GW of large-scale wind and solar capacity by 2035.

Senvion India Secures 210 MW Wind Turbine Order

New Jersey Approves Invenergy's Extension for Wind Farm

Senvion India announced it secured an order from Continuum Green Energy for 50 Wind Turbine Generators (WTGs) totaling 210 MW, set for deployment in Gujarat or Karnataka starting April 2026. The agreement allows for an additional 50 units. The advanced 4.2M160 turbines promise enhanced efficiency and reduced operational costs, emphasizing Senvion's commitment to Renewable Energy.

The New Jersey Board of Public Utilities has granted Invenergy an extension until December 20, 2024, to finalize its power purchase agreement for the 2.4GW Leading Light offshore wind farm. Invenergy requested more time to secure a turbine supplier after the cancellation of the GE Haliade-X and a Siemens Gamesa price increase. The NJBPU remains committed to New Jersey's Offshore Wind goals.
