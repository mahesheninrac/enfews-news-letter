Europe’s Green Steel Shift: Impact on MENA and Turkey

 

We’re excited to share our latest McCloskey by OPIS, A Dow Jones Company blog post, Europe’s Green Steel Shift: Impact on MENA and Turkey, exploring how the EU’s decarbonization policies are reshaping the global steel industry and what it means for steel producers in the region.

 

The Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM), set to take effect in 2026, will impose levies on carbon-intensive steel imports, driving a shift toward low-carbon production. European steelmakers are already investing in green hydrogen, direct reduced iron (DRI), and electric arc furnace (EAF) technologies to meet new emissions standards.

 

Over the next decade, more than 70 million tonnes of low-carbon steel capacity will replace traditional methods, setting new industry benchmarks. As a result, steel exporters in MENA and Turkey must adapt quickly to maintain their position in the European market. Countries like China and India are already scaling up green steel initiatives to stay competitive.
